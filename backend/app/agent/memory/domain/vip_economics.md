# VIP Player Economics

## P&L Waterfall

A VIP player's profitability follows this waterfall:

1. **Nominal Volume** → The total amount the player wagers (e.g., $10M/month for a Whale)
2. **Weighted Volume** → Adjusted by game mix. High-RTP games (99%+) contribute less per dollar than lower-RTP games
3. **GGR** → Weighted volume x effective edge. For default VIP mix, effective GGR rate is ~1.625% of nominal
4. **Ops Costs** → Casino ops (10% of casino GGR) + Sportsbook ops (11% of SB GGR)
5. **NGR Before Promos** → GGR minus ops costs
6. **Affiliate Cost** → 25% of NGR before promos (default)
7. **Bonuses** → Level-up (5%), Reload (10%), Weekly (3%), Monthly (7%), Lossback (30%) — all as % of GGR
8. **NGR After Promos** → The bottom line

## What Drives VIP Margins

- **Wager mix**: More sportsbook or low-RTP games = higher GGR per dollar wagered
- **Lossback %**: The single biggest lever. 30% lossback on GGR is the largest cost item
- **Affiliate cost**: At 25% of NGR, this is a major margin drain
- **Bonus stacking**: Multiple bonus types compound. Total bonus load can exceed 55% of GGR
- **Volume**: Higher volume doesn't change margins (linear model), but absolute profit scales

## Key Insight

With default assumptions, a Whale generates ~$162,500 GGR but only ~$42,394 NGR after affiliate — and that's before bonuses. The bonus load (especially lossback) determines whether the player is profitable.
